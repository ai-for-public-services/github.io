Section 3: When you have received approval to recruit

Ensure you have a vacant position number

Speak to your HR department to ensure you have a vacant position number within your team. Position numbers help to control staffing resource and make sure that vacancy holders cannot appoint to posts they do not have budget for.

You‚Äôll need the position number to set up a new starter on the Shared Services system so that they are issued with a staff number and are paid correctly and on time.

 

Think about role requirements and candidate type

Understand your role requirements and the type of candidate you are looking for. Departmental Resourcing Group (DRG) can then recommend the best recruitment strategy to secure the right person for your role.

Read the guidance on Success Profiles, so that you understand how to assess candidates against the following criteria:

  • Behaviours
  • Experience
  • Technical
  • Ability
  • Strengths

You also need to understand the tests and assessments available and when to use them to select candidates.   

You should also:

  • ensure your role profile is up to date and relevant
  • ensure you‚Äôve secured approval from your HR department for allowances for working on call or night shifts, for example
  • ensure you‚Äôve secured approval from your HR department for media spend
  • start thinking about panel members for the sift and interviews
  • ensure all panel members (including you) have completed the mandatory training:
    • Civil Service Expectations
    • Success Profiles: sifting and interviewing
  • consider the required level of clearance for the role. See national security vetting: clearance levels for further information
  • understand the nationality requirements for recruiting within the Civil Service
  • ensure you‚Äôve secured HR approval if you‚Äôre advertising a role that can be filled by UK nationals only (this is known as a reserved post)
  • ensure you‚Äôve secured HR approval if the role you‚Äôre advertising excludes working part-time hours
  • consider whether the role would merit visa sponsorship. If not, this must be explicitly stated in your job advert

 

Understand how the application form works

Candidates can find out about working for Dpt and its executive agencies at Careers at the Department and they can apply for roles online via Civil Service Jobs. In some cases, campaigns are run through recruitment agencies and applications will be submitted directly to that agency.

Candidates complete a 2-part application when they apply for a job. In the first section (short application), they provide their personal details and answer questions about:

  • their eligibility
  • any required mandatory qualifications, memberships, licences and languages
  • their right to work in the UK

If you‚Äôre running a volume campaign, you might also want to include an online assessment from the Ability element of Success Profiles to test the core abilities that underpin the role.

If candidates fail the short application, the application is rejected and they cannot continue.

If they‚Äôre successful, they‚Äôll automatically be able to complete the full application.

 

Think about how to make your recruitment campaign inclusive

Make a commitment to inclusive recruitment throughout your campaign. Check your intranet to understand your agency‚Äôs diversity objectives.

Use gender neutral language in your job advert and think about options that will make a significant difference to job applicants, such as offering:

  • flexible working
  • training or mentoring in the role

Speak to your Diversity and Inclusivity or HR team about how you can make your role inclusive to all.

The job specification or person requirements of a vacancy must not exclude disabled people from applying for a job. However, some jobs may have an essential requirement that cannot be met with a reasonable adjustment in the workplace. For example, a vacancy for a van driver says the person must be able to drive. This might exclude some disabled people, but it is an essential part of the job and needs to be made clear in the job advert and role profile.

Read Recruitment and disabled people for further information.

At application or interview stage, some candidates may need an adjustment to allow them to be considered for the job. You must make adjustments if they‚Äôre reasonable. For example, allowing:

  • extra time in assessments
  • candidates to complete a written test using a computer
  • wheelchair users to have their interview on the ground floor

Each candidate‚Äôs request should be handled individually and sensitively. Your HR department will advise you on how to arrange reasonable adjustments where possible.

Read Providing reasonable adjustments for sift and interview for more information.

 

Civil Service recruitment schemes that promote diversity and equal opportunities

There are 3 Civil Service recruitment schemes to help promote diversity and equal opportunities:

  • Disability Confident scheme (DCS)
  • Great Place to Work for Veterans (GPTW)
  • Redeployment Interview Scheme (RIS)

For each of these schemes, candidates who are eligible and achieve the minimum selection criteria and essential requirements for the role will progress to the next stage of selection. They will be flagged during the sift stage of the application process.

Candidates should be given guidance on how to apply for each of these schemes in the job advert and application form.

 

Disability Confident scheme

To be eligible to apply under the Disability Confident scheme (DCS), candidates must have a disability defined by the Equality Act 2010.

 

A Great Place to Work for Veterans initiative

To be eligible to apply under the Great Place to Work for Veterans (GPTW) initiative, candidates must:

  • have served for at least one year in Her Majesty‚Äôs Armed Forces (as a Regular or Reserve) ‚Äì this includes time spent training
  • be in transition from, or ceased to be a member of, HM Armed Forces
  • not already be a civil servant, or be employed by a Civil Service Commission accredited public body

 

Redeployment Interview Scheme

Under the Redeployment Interview Scheme (RIS), Civil Service employers can offer an interview to a fair and proportionate number of civil servants who are at risk of redundancy.

For example, a person may be at risk if their work area has launched a voluntary or compulsory redundancy scheme that they are eligible for, or if they are aware that their employer has begun consultation on how to reduce staff in their immediate team or work area.

To be eligible to apply under RIS, candidates must:

  • have good reason to believe that they are likely to be made redundant in the near future
  • be applying for a job at the same grade as, or lower than, their current job

 

Prison Leavers

Prison leavers may not realise that roles within the Civil Service are available to them.

They might be concerned that they need to declare an offence at an early stage and will be immediately sifted out. However, their applications are considered alongside others in the same fair and open recruitment process. Criminal convictions are only asked about once a provisional job offer has been made and accepted.

See Prison Leaver Recruitment for more information and exempt offences.

 

Decide whether you want to advertise externally, across government or internally

Most Civil Service roles are advertised either externally or across all government departments.

 

Reasons to advertise externally

Roles advertised outside of the Civil Service:

  • give everyone the opportunity to compete for roles in the Civil Service, ensuring we have the most skilled and capable people delivering national priorities
  • support Dpt in achieving its Diversity and Inclusion agenda by opening our vacancies to a wider demographic

 

Reasons to advertise across government

Roles advertised across government are available to employees in all departments within the Civil Service at grades AA to Grade 6. The aim is to recruit for skills predominantly, or almost exclusively, found among existing civil servants.

 

Reasons to advertise internally

Roles that are advertised internally are only available to employees in your department. You must be able to prove that the skillset required is exclusive to your department only. You could advertise an internal role as a managed move within your department.

 

Complete the vacancy notification tool

Complete the vacancy notification tool to raise a request for each vacancy you have. There are separate notification tools for each agency:

  • Dptc and ATE EOI
  • Dptc and ATE recruitment campaign
  • Dptc and ATE reserve list request
  • DADA recruitment campaign
  • DADA reserve list request
  • DVSA: this link will be issued to DVSA vacancy holders once internal approvals have been completed
  • MCB vacancy notification
  • BCA vacancy notification

Once you‚Äôve completed and submitted the vacancy notification tool, a DRG advisor will be in contact within 2 working days to arrange an early engagement call.

 

Restructuring, redeployment and redundancy

Employees on the restructuring, redeployment and redundancy‚ÄØ(RRR) register are given priority to submit an interest in a role before it‚Äôs advertised. 

Before your early engagement call is scheduled, DRG will check against the Dpt‚ÄØRRR register to see if there are any matches for your role. 

If there are, individuals will be given 48 hours to express an interest in the position. ‚ÄØ 

After considering an employee‚Äôs expression of interest, complete and send the relevant feedback form to the employee, their line manager and DRG to either offer them the role or explain why they‚Äôve been unsuccessful in this instance. 

If you receive a match that you assess as unsuitable and an employee challenges your decision not to appoint them, your recruitment campaign planning cannot start until the issue has been resolved to the satisfaction of the applicant and their line manager as outlined in the RRR policy: redeployment challenge process.‚ÄØ 

If there are no matches, you can schedule an early engagement call to plan your recruitment campaign. 

Once 48 hours has lapsed, employees on the RRR register will no longer be matched to your vacancy. However, if they apply for a role via Civil Service Jobs or Careers at Department for Transport, there is an option for them to be interviewed under the‚ÄØRedeployment Interview Scheme (RIS).
