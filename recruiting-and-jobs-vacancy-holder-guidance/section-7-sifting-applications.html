Section 7: Sifting applications

You‚Äôll receive an email from Government Recruitment Services (GRS) to let you know that your campaign has closed and your candidates are ready to sift. Alternatively, you can start sifting as soon as full applications are submitted rather than waiting until the campaign has closed.

You will be advised if any candidates have requested reasonable adjustments, which you can discuss with your HR department.

 

Download and circulate your applications

Once your advert has closed, log into Oleeo vX to download the applications ready for sifting. These will show the ‚ÄòSift Application‚Äô status.

Candidates who have requested a reasonable adjustment will be flagged as ‚ÄòHelp Required‚Äô. GRS will review these requests and update the status to ‚ÄòSift Application‚Äô.

Send the applications to the sift panel, ensuring each member has a copy of the scoring matrix. Each sift panel member should carry out their sift independently. Then you‚Äôll need to meet and moderate the scores.

To support the principles of fair and open competition, you will be unable to see an applicant‚Äôs personal details until their sift results have been issued to candidates. Each applicant is tracked by an ID number instead of their name.

 

How to score applications

The departmental approach to scoring applications is outlined in the tables below. Behaviours, Technical and Experience elements score 1 to 7 with a minimum acceptable score of 4.

If you‚Äôre expecting a large number of applications for your role, you can raise the minimum acceptable score in one or more of the Success Profiles being assessed. Candidates who have applied under any of the Civil Service recruitment schemes still only have to score a minimum of 4 to be progressed to the next stage.

In exceptional cases (if you‚Äôre expecting very few applications for your role, for example), you may be willing for a candidate to score a 3 in one of the elements to progress. If so, discuss this with your DRG advisor in your early engagement call, so that you can redefine the criteria for scoring a 4. Alternatively, you could agree a benchmark upfront that allows for one score of 3.

Whatever you decide on, the essential criteria must be agreed between sift and interview panel members ahead of the recruitment process or in your early engagement call, so that you‚Äôre all clear on what you‚Äôre assessing and what ‚Äòacceptable‚Äô looks like.

 

Any amendments to the criteria or the scoring benchmarks can only be applied before the selection process begins and not during or after the process, as this could be a breach of the recruitment principles.

 

Dpt scoring system for Behaviours, Technical and Experience elements

Score Criteria
1. Not demonstrated No positive evidence and/or substantial negative evidence demonstrated.
2. Minimal demonstration Limited positive evidence and/or mainly negative evidence demonstrated.
3. Moderate demonstration Moderate positive evidence but some negative evidence demonstrated.
4. Acceptable demonstration Adequate positive evidence and any negative evidence would not cause concern.
5. Good demonstration Substantial positive evidence demonstrated.
6. Strong demonstration Substantial positive evidence. Includes some evidence of exceeding expectations at this level.
7. Outstanding demonstration The evidence provided wholly exceeds expectation at this level.

 

How to score CVs and personal statements

When sifting by CV and personal statement, Oleeo vX gives you the option to score candidates between 1 and 100. This is to cater for all government departments using Oleeo vX as their ATS platform.

Dpt scoring criteria, however, remains between 1 and 7.

 

Any scores higher than 7 will be rejected by GRS during its quality assurance process.

 

If you ask applicants for a CV and personal statement, combine the 2 elements to give one overall score of 1 to 7.

When uploading your results to Oleeo vX, both elements require a rating. To do this:

  • input the total rating awarded into the first element
  • select ‚Äò0 ‚Äì not assessed‚Äô for the second element

The candidate will only see the overall rating and not the breakdown against the individual elements. They will not be able to see the outcome.

Scoring a personal statement

Wording in advert

When considering your skills and experience, tailor your personal statement to provide evidence of when you have:

  • planned, managed and implemented a project or programme from start to finish
  • motivated a team by giving enthusiastic and encouraging messages about priorities, objectives and expectations
  • influenced and engaged with a wide range of stakeholders
  • adopted clear processes and standards for managing performance at all levels
