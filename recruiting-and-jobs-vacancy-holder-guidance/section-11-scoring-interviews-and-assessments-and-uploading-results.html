Section 11: Scoring interviews and assessments and uploading results

Following the interviews, candidates will be given a score for each of the elements they were assessed on. These scores will be subject to a minimum pass mark for the interview, which candidates must meet to be successful and offered a role or be placed on a reserve list.

Candidates must be scored against the Success Profiles stated in the job advert.

The following table provides an example of how the minimum score would be calculated if 3 Success Profile elements were assessed at interview.

Scoring Behaviour questions

All Behaviours must have a minimum ‚Äòfinal score‚Äô of 4 to pass.

If the pass mark of 28 is achieved but the candidate has not met the minimum requirement of 4 per Behaviour, they would be rejected.

If you have more one question per Behaviour, add the scores per Behaviour together and divide the total by the number of questions. Round up (0.5 and above) or down (0.4 and below) to arrive at the ‚Äòfinal score‚Äô per Behaviour.

 

Scoring Experience questions

In Oleeo vX, there is only space for one score for Experience.

For consistency across Dpt, the scores will need to be recorded as a total and not averaged.

For example, there could be 3 Experience questions asked as part of the interview. The scores would be added up, so the total minimum pass mark would be 12 (3 x 4).

In the ‚ÄòExperience Score Description‚Äô section in Oleeo vX, the detail would need to be included for each candidate. For example:

  • Experience Q1 score = 4
  • Experience Q2 score = 4
  • Experience Q3 score = 4
  • Experience total = 12

If the candidate scored below a 4 in any of the questions, they would not progress. Their score can be seen in the description to justify this. You also need to include this information in the Interview Outcome Report, so that Government Recruitment Services (GRS) can process the results without confusion.

For example: For Experience, there were 3 questions, therefore, the minimum pass mark for experience is 12. Candidates who scored below a 4 in any of the Experience questions are not successful. The breakdown of their scores in each question can be seen in the ‚ÄòExperience Score Description‚Äô.

 

Scoring Strength questions

Strength questions are scored from 1 to 4 with a minimum acceptable score of 2.

 

Dpt scoring system for the Strength element

Score Criteria
1. Weakness ‚Äì lower engagement, lower capability, lower use. A behaviour a candidate does not enjoy doing and lacks the ability to do. Includes a poor response, lack of engagement, going off topic or other displacement activity.
2. Learned behaviour ‚Äì lower engagement, higher capability, some use. When a candidate can do something well, but they find it draining. They complete a task because they must rather than through preference.
3. Potential strength ‚Äì higher engagement, some capability, some use. When a candidate shows willingness, desire and motivation to demonstrate, and shows some capability, but may not yet be fully developed.
4. Strength ‚Äì higher engagement, higher capability, higher use. A combination of what someone enjoys doing and what they‚Äôre good at. Candidates demonstrating strengths do so naturally and consistently. They are at their peak and easy to spot.

 

Scoring written tests, presentations and role play

For assessment elements such as written exercises, presentation or role play, record the scores under the relevant Success Profile linked to that particular assessment. If the Success Profile was assessed through more than one method, such as in an interview question and a presentation, give the average score.

When uploading your results to Oleeo vX, you will be asked: ‚ÄòHow many additional assessments (such as a presentation or e-tray exercise) did the selection event involve?‚Äô Enter the number of assessments completed.

You‚Äôll then be asked for the ‚ÄòName of assessment‚Äô. Enter presentation, written exercise or role play. When prompted to provide a score, select ‚Äò0 ‚Äì not assessed‚Äô. 

In the assessments comments section, enter the following, for example: ‚ÄòThe presentation, written exercise or role play was scored against the Behaviours Leadership and Seeing the Big Picture. The scores have been recorded under the relevant Behaviour/Experience section above.‚Äô

 

Same scoring candidates: rationale for offer and reserve list order

To make it easier to rank candidates in merit order, it is useful to agree a lead Success Profile element between panel members before the sift and interview stages.

The score for this element will take precedence over the other elements in the case of candidates attaining the same overall score.

For those scoring the same mark, include a justification for offering one individual the position and placing others on a reserve list in merit order. A clear justifiable rationale must be identified to provide distinction between candidates, such as one candidate scores the highest in one of the following:

  • the lead Success Profile element
  • the lead Behaviour
  • Behavioural ranking: common for large campaigns where many candidates may have the same score for the lead Behaviour. The vacancy holder would rank the Behaviours in the following order, for example:
    • first ‚Äì Leadership
    • second ‚Äì Seeing the Big Picture
    • third ‚Äì Developing Self and Others
    • fourth ‚Äì Making Effective Decisions
  • the lead Technical skill from any Professional Framework

There are many other options available, but these are the most common. The rationale must be related to the role profile (or the candidate pack in the case of DVSA) and cannot be subjective. For example, you cannot rank one candidate over another for enthusiasm, interest in role or ‚Äòfit‚Äô.

 

Uploading interview results to Oleeo vX

Once the interview scores have been agreed by your panel, complete the interview evaluation form in Oleeo vX using the individual candidate record or the bulk upload form.

You‚Äôll need to provide feedback at this stage. Ensure that it is objective, clear, comprehensive and professional.

You‚Äôll be prompted to state the position in which the candidate should appear on the reserve list. Candidates must be ranked in merit order, which means the highest scoring candidate comes first. If candidates have the same overall score, see Same scoring candidates: rationale for offer and reserve list order.

Successful candidates will move to ‚ÄòSelected for Offer‚Äô status but will not be automatically emailed.

Complete an Interview Outcome Report via the ‚ÄòInterview Results Ready‚Äô button. This will need to include the pass marks and rationale for the overall outcome of candidates who have been awarded the same score.

Within 2 working days, GRS will:

  • email all candidates informing them of their outcome
  • forward any feedback from the panel
  • send a provisional offer to the successful candidate(s)

 

All panel papers should be retained by the Chair for 3 months for audit purposes. You are responsible for ensuring that all documents are retained correctly and can be made available if requested by Departmental Resourcing Group (DRG).

 

For existing civil servants from other government departments

After receiving an email from GRS confirming their provisional job offer, an other government department (OGD) candidate will receive a follow-up email containing the staff transfer form, also known as the Civil Service Employee Transfer (CSET) form.

This covers HR, payroll and security clearance transfer.

The candidate needs to complete part A-1 of the CSET and send it to you with their payslips.

Check the salary entitlement for the candidate, which will help you to complete the New Entrant Notification (NEN), along with details of their current security clearance level.

When updating the candidate status on Oleeo vX, you‚Äôll be asked if security checks are required. If the candidate‚Äôs stated level of security is higher than the level required for your post, select ‚Äòno‚Äô.

Select ‚Äòyes‚Äô if the role requires a higher clearance than the candidate holds, or the candidate has said no to the following question in Part A-1 of the transfer form: ‚ÄúCan your current department confirm that BPSS checks have been conducted within the last 5 years?‚Äù

You need to confirm salary details with your candidate by email.

The candidate needs to ensure that all 4 sections of the CSET are completed. Further guidance is available at Guidance for civil servants: How to move jobs between departments and agencies.
