Section 4: Planning your recruitment campaign ‚Äì early engagement call

An early engagement call takes place between the vacancy holder and a Departmental Resourcing Group (DRG) advisor. You can invite colleagues who are supporting you with your recruitment campaign or your HR department/resourcing team.

DRG will advise on the best methods to assess and attract the right candidates. You‚Äôll discuss:

  • your role in detail
  • the ideal candidate
  • attraction methods, which could include open days or virtual events
  • assessment types, such as written exercise, presentation or role play, at sift and interview, or online tests at the short application stage
  • launch, sift, interview and closing dates

You should consider the following ahead of your call:

  • the key responsibilities and deliverables of the role
  • the specific skills and experiences the candidate will need to be successful in the role
  • how you want to advertise the role and what budget has been secured for this if your role is going external
  • your campaign timelines
  • interview and assessment accommodation
  • any previous recruitment successes that would be applicable to your campaign
  • working patterns the role could facilitate. If, the role was part-time, for example, what is the required minimum number of hours?

If you‚Äôre expecting very few applications for your role or feel that, for any other reason, you may need to lower the minimum acceptable score in one or more of the Success Profiles being assessed, discuss this with your DRG advisor.

The essential criteria must be agreed between sift and interview panel members ahead of the recruitment process or in your early engagement call, so that you‚Äôre all clear on what you‚Äôre assessing and what ‚Äòacceptable‚Äô looks like.

For more information, see How to score applications.

 

Professions and external supplier options

DRG has access to the appropriate professional competency framework, including the likes of Government Digital and Data Profession (DDaT) and Operational Delivery. The DRG team can support you with the design of sifting and assessment tools, customised to the requirements within your profession.

 

External supplier options

Sometimes you will need external support to assist in running hard-to-fill recruitment campaigns.

In such cases, ensure you have the budget secured to cover the costs of any product or service you plan to buy in. Plan ahead and discuss this with your commercial and HR teams to allow sufficient time for the procurement process as part of your campaign plan. It‚Äôs important that DRG is included in the initial briefing call you have with the chosen supplier to ensure they‚Äôre aware of the Dpt recruitment process, such as anonymisation at sift. Discuss this in your early engagement call.

DRG will still be responsible for providing you with recommendations and materials for the final interview and assessment stage.

For further information about external supplier options for permanent recruitment, visit:‚ÄØ

  • Crown Commercial Service Permanent Recruitment 2
  • Crown Commercial Services Executive and Non-Executive Recruitment Services

 

External advertising

DRG will support advertising your recruitment campaign by posting job roles on:

  • Dpt Careers website
  • Facebook and X
  • LinkedIn
  • Inclusive Jobs
  • OneFinance Intranet (finance roles only)
  • Rail Delivery Group (rail roles only)

Other options might be available. Discuss this with your DRG advisor. Landing pages can be created on the Dpt Careers website for larger campaigns and for vacancy holders with drop-in or virtual events.

These options are free of charge.‚ÄØ 

Once your role has been advertised on social media and LinkedIn, share the posts on your own accounts.

 

Paid media

If your campaign is being advertised externally, and your role is specialised or hard to fill, your DRG advisor will discuss the option of using paid media to advertise your vacancy further afield.

DRG uses a third-party supplier that provides advice and recommendations on where and how to advertise external campaigns.

If you want a quote for paid media, raise a purchase order and give this to your DRG advisor, who will contact Government Recruitment Services (GRS) with details of your role. GRS will engage with the supplier on your behalf.

You will be charged for the quote even if you do not go ahead, so ensure this is the right option for you.

For further information on purchase orders, contact your HR Business Partner or HR department.

You‚Äôll then receive a media schedule with recommended websites/media and costings. You need to review this and decide if you would like to proceed with any of the recommendations. You can also specify websites/media you would like costing for.

All media is quoted for a 4-week period regardless of how long the campaign is being advertised for. To get value for money when using paid media, aim to keep the role open for 4 weeks as opposed to the usual 2 weeks. The supplier can also track the media you use to provide a report on how effective it has been, which will help you to make an informed decision for similar roles in the future. This will incur an additional cost and time to set up.‚ÄØ 

 

After the early engagement call

After the call, your DRG advisor will complete and send you your recruitment campaign documents.

You need to review or complete these before the campaign can go live on Civil Service Jobs.

For most campaigns, the documents include:

 

Advert

The advert will include:

  • a job description
  • a person specification
  • important details about your role
  • information on how the candidate can apply
  • the Success Profile elements that will be assessed
  • salary and applicable allowances (for being on call or working night shifts, for example)
  • significant dates

Your role profile will also be attached to the advert when the vacancy goes live.

For DVSA and VCA campaigns, a candidate pack will be attached instead of a role profile. See the following example of a candidate pack for a driving examiner.

All vacancies are published on Civil Service Jobs and Find a job.

Candidates are informed in the advert that feedback will only be provided if they attend an interview or assessment and it is not provided at sift.

 

Recruitment plan

Your DRG advisor will provide you with a summary of your discussion in a recruitment plan. This will include key dates, sift and interview methods and media plans.

You need to:

  • review the recruitment plan
  • make suggested improvements or confirm you are happy with it
  • respond by the deadline date to avoid any delays

If there are any changes, DRG will send you a revised recruitment plan.

To make sure your campaign meets its deadlines, ensure that all panel members schedule time for sifting, interviewing and completing the associated admin.

 

Sift and interview panel form

You‚Äôll be asked to confirm who your panel members are at the sift and interview stages to ensure all requirements have been met. For more information, read How sift and interview panels work.

 

CV and personal statement scoring matrix

If you‚Äôre using a CV and/or personal statement as part of the sift stage of your campaign, DRG will send you a matrix and accompanying guidance for scoring your candidates.

In terms of what you ask candidates to evidence in their CV or personal statement, you need to be upfront and specific. Provide a clear list of criteria (based on skills and experience). You cannot ask candidates to evidence Civil Service Behaviours in their CV or personal statement, as this request is too vague.

DRG can help you to create a list of essential criteria based on the Experience or Technical elements of the Success Profiles framework. See Recruitment campaign assessment types for sift and interview for further information.

 

Interview pack

If Behaviour and/or Strength questions form part of your interview, you‚Äôll receive an interview pack with a script for you to follow and a selection of Behaviour/Strength questions to choose from. DRG will select these based on your role and the Behaviours and Strengths being assessed.

If you want to write your own Behavioural Situational, Experience or Technical questions, you need to provide these within the interview pack, along with a model answer for each question you have created. DRG will provide guidance. You‚Äôll also find examples in:

  • recruitment campaign assessment types for sift and interview
  • guidance on writing interview questions

Avoid leading questions, and those which require knowledge of your departmental activities. Pay particular attention to whether the question would give an internal candidate advantage over an external one.

Once you‚Äôve written your questions, send them to your DRG advisor for approval ahead of the interviews. This is to ensure the questions are fair, open and relevant to the role.

If using an assessment (such as a presentation, written exercise or role play) as part of your campaign, DRG will send you the following documents to complete:

  • candidate brief: this will be given to the candidate and contains instructions on what they need to do as part of the assessment
  • assessor guide: shows how to score the candidate and will be linked to Success Profiles
  • administrator instructions: includes assessment materials required on the day, such as a flipchart, laptop for assessment or notepads. Also includes instructions for panel members on their specific roles in the interview
  • actor brief: only needs to be completed if using a role play and will include instructions for the actor
