Section 13: Pre-employment checks

Once the right to work checks have been completed, the pre-employment checks (PECs) process starts.

 

Do not agree a start date until all pre-employment checks have been completed.

 

Government Recruitment Services (GRS) invites candidates to complete PECs forms via Oleeo vX. The number of forms will depend on the candidate‚Äôs circumstances and the checks specified in your vacancy.

The candidate may also be asked to upload a document to Oleeo vX to confirm their nationality for the purposes of the Civil Service nationality rules for recruitment.

As a vacancy holder, you need to:

  • keep in touch with the candidate to support them through the pre-employment process
  • check the status of your candidate regularly on Oleeo vX to make sure all actions have been completed
  • ensure that all correspondence and pre-employment queries from GRS are dealt with immediately to avoid delay

 

Understanding pre-employment checks

GRS carries out all pre-employment checks. Completion for most candidates can take up to 12 days. However, this is dependent on the clearance level of the role and the candidate‚Äôs background and circumstances, such as:

  • age
  • how many times they‚Äôve moved home
  • where they‚Äôve travelled
  • previous employment
  • family and friends

The criteria for most checks is set out in the Baseline Personnel Security Standard (BPSS).

Pre-employment checks can include:

  • criminal records checks
  • employment history checks
  • checks to see if a candidate has a 6 month or more gap in their employment history in the past 3 years, to determine when references are needed
  • self-employment checks
  • checks to identify any areas of concern or conflicts of interest
  • asking overseas candidates to provide information on time spent out of the country for the past 3 years
  • a previous Civil Service check
  • an internal fraud in government check
  • a pre-employment health screening ‚Äì there are 7 questions as part of this and if the candidate answers ‚Äòyes‚Äô to any question, they will be referred for a more detailed health or medical check
  • a pension check (for candidates who are not current civil servants)
  • national security vetting (NSV) where applicable
  • other government department (OD) checks where applicable
  • conflict of interest (social media check)

Read the GRS guidance on documents that can be used for pre-employment checks.

 

How the different pre-employment checks work

The vetting team or your HR department will help you decide which of the following checks you require for your candidate.

 

Criminal record checks

The most common criminal record check providers that GRS uses are:

  • Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) for roles based in England and Wales
  • Disclosure Scotland for roles based in Scotland
  • Access NI for roles based in Northern Ireland

There are 3 levels of criminal record check available ‚Äì Basic, Standard and Enhanced.

The level of check required and the location where the role is based determine which ID documents a candidate is required to provide.

Read the guidance on DBS checks and processes involved.

 

Eligibility for digital criminal record checks

The pre-employment checking forms are used to confirm if a digital criminal record check, using IDVT, is appropriate for a candidate.

To conduct a basic level digital criminal record check, the candidate must have (one of the following):

  • a valid UK or Irish passport or Irish passport card
  • a biometric passport that meets the ICAO specifications for e-passports
  • an EU biometric ID card

To conduct a standard or enhanced level digital criminal record check, the candidate must have a UK, EU or EEA driving licence and (one of the following):

  • a valid UK or Irish passport or Irish passport card
  • a biometric passport that meets the ICAO specifications for e-passports
  • an EU biometric ID card

All candidates also need to provide a valid document as proof of their current address.

 

Employment history checks

As part of the BPSS, GRS will check a candidate‚Äôs employment history. GRS staff will verify this electronically, checking the candidate‚Äôs past 3 years of employment history. This minimises the need to request references.

 

Reference checks

If a candidate has a gap of 6 months or more in their employment history, references are sought. Acceptable references include:

  • personal references
  • employer references
  • academic references

GRS is unable to accept references from anyone who is related to the candidate or has known the candidate for less than 3 years.

 

Self-employment checks

Where periods of self-employment are identified on a candidate‚Äôs pre-employment forms, checks are carried out. This is to ensure the candidate‚Äôs business was properly conducted and whether there are any potential red flags or conflicts of interest. If GRS identifies an area of concern, you will be alerted, so you can make an informed decision on whether to employ the candidate.

 

Overseas checks

Candidates are asked to provide information on time spent out of the country for the past 3 years. Checks are performed to ensure there are no conflicts of interest for employment in the Civil Service. A lack of UK residency is not an automatic bar to employment, but GRS does need to be in receipt of documentary evidence that satisfies the overseas check.

 

Previous Civil Service checks

Candidates are asked if they‚Äôve ever been dismissed from a Civil Service role. If the answer is yes, GRS highlights any areas of concern to the vacancy holder. This allows you to make a risk-based decision on employing the individual. 

 

Internal fraud in government checks

The Internal Fraud Database (IFD) contains a record of anyone dismissed for fraud or dishonesty offences. This check does not form part of the BPSS but GRS checks all external candidates against the database. If GRS receives a result on this search, the candidate‚Äôs pre-employment checks are automatically failed.

 

Pre-employment health screening

Health checks do not form part of the BPSS but are undertaken by GRS to make sure a candidate is fit and well to work. The check allows GRS to advise of any workplace adjustments that may be needed ahead of a candidate taking up their role.

 

Pension checks (for candidates who are not current civil servants)

As part of pre-employment checks, all candidates who are not current civil servants are asked to complete a pension questionnaire. This will establish if they have made any previous Civil Service or public service pension contributions.

The form is reviewed by GRS to establish the correct pension scheme for the candidate. A pension choices starter pack will be issued to the candidate.

For some candidates, a pension abatement may apply if they are in receipt of a Civil Service pension, returning to a Civil Service organisation and re-enrolled into a Civil Service pension scheme.

National security vetting checks

Everyone employed in the Civil Service is subject to BPSS checks. For roles that involve sensitive issues, national security vetting (NSV) checks will apply.

There are 3 levels of vetting and these take longer to conduct than BPSS checks. The average wait times are:

  • Counter Terrorist Check (CTC): 37 working days
  • Security Check (SC): 37 working days
  • Developed Vetting Check (DV): 125 working days

These timeframes are a guideline only. In some instances, checks may take longer than anticipated. Unless United Kingdom Security Vetting (UKSV) needs more information from you, you will not receive any updates until your application has been completed.

The minimum continuous residency criteria when applying for NSV clearance is:

  • CTC: 3 years continuous residency in the UK
  • SC: 5 years continuous residency in the UK
  • DV: 10 years continuous residency in the UK

GRS works alongside the national security vetting provider to carry out the checks. For more information, read United Kingdom Security Vetting: clearance levels.

If your candidate already holds NSV at a level that is equal to or higher than the requirement for the new role, email GRS on pre-employmentchecks.grs@cabinetoffice.gov.uk as it may be possible to transfer their current clearance into Dpt and speed up the pre-employment checks process. Clearances given are linked to the role that the candidate is in at the time and not to the individual themselves.

 

Do not agree a start date until all pre-employment checks have been completed, even if the candidate already holds NSV.

 

Other department (OD) checks

Where a candidate is transferring to Dpt from an OD:

  • right to work checks are recommended
  • the candidate‚Äôs national security vetting (CTC, CS or DV) level can be transferred if they hold clearance at a level that is equal to or higher than the requirement for their new role
  • a stripped back version of the BPSS check (a criminal record check only) is required if the candidate does not already hold a level of national security vetting

 

Social media and conflict of interest checks

Once a candidate‚Äôs status changes to ‚ÄòPre-Employment Checks‚Äô in Oleeo vX, Departmental Resourcing Group (DRG) sends that candidate‚Äôs information to Capita to perform social media and conflict of interest checks. Capita will then contact the candidate. The checks cannot start until the candidate replies to Capita to consent to them. It usually takes 2 to 8 days working days for the checks to be completed. Any adverse findings are sent to DRG for review. The recruitment process is paused until the review is complete.

When a candidate‚Äôs social media and conflict of interest checks are complete, DRG adds the date and completion certificate to Oleeo vX.

 

Using Oleeo vX to keep track of pre-employment checks

Oleeo vX provides live updates and timestamps for each stage of the checking process. A traffic light system on the dashboard shows the status of each check.

For example, where checks are referenced in Oleeo vX:

  • blue means not required
  • yellow means outstanding
  • green means passed
  • red means refer to risk assessment

GRS will email you with details of any visa end dates, right to work restrictions or health related adjustments that may need to be considered. You‚Äôll have an opportunity to review any flagged risks and either accept or decline proceeding with the candidate in question through Oleeo vX. If you reject the risk assessment, the candidate will fail their pre-employment checks. If you accept the risk assessment, GRS will continue with the remaining checks. If you need advice, speak to your manager and/or HR department.

Further guidance on using Oleeo vX is available on GOV.UK.

 

Visa sponsorship

Dpt is an approved visa sponsor for skilled workers. At the pre-employment check stage, GRS will notify the vacancy holder if a successful candidate requires visa sponsorship. See guidance on your intranet and contact your HR department for advice.

If the role does not offer sponsorship, this needs to be stated in your job advert.
